Laser Hair Removal

Full body laser hair removal is a cutting-edge cosmetic procedure that uses advanced laser technology to permanently reduce or eliminate unwanted body hair. Unlike traditional methods like shaving or waxing, which provide temporary relief, laser hair removal offers a more permanent solution to the age-old problem of unwanted hair.

Laser hair removal works by targeting the hair follicles with concentrated beams of light. The pigment in the hair follicles absorbs the laser energy, which then damages the follicles, inhibiting future hair growth. It’s like putting a stop sign in front of your hair’s growth path. Over time, with multiple sessions, you can achieve smooth and hair-free skin.

Full body laser hair removal

at a Glance

Procedure Time: The duration of a full-body laser hair removal session can vary depending on the size of the treatment area and the specific technology used. On average, it can take anywhere from 1 to 3 hours per session. Smaller areas like the underarms or upper lip may take less time, while larger areas like the legs or back may require more time.

Number of Treatments: Achieving the desired results typically requires multiple treatment sessions. Most people undergo a series of sessions spaced several weeks apart. The exact number of sessions needed varies from person to person, but a common range is 6 to 8 sessions. Some individuals may require more, while others may need fewer sessions.

Duration of Results: Laser hair removal offers long-lasting results. After completing the recommended series of sessions, many individuals experience a significant reduction in hair growth. However, it’s important to note that results can vary from person to person. Some people may enjoy hair-free or nearly hair-free skin for several years, while others may experience some regrowth over time. Periodic maintenance sessions may be required to sustain the results.

Preparing for Your Full Body

Laser Hair Removal Session

Before your laser hair removal session, there are a few essential steps you should follow to ensure the best results and a comfortable experience. Here’s what you need to do:

Shave the Treatment Area: It’s essential to shave the treatment area a day or two before your appointment. This helps the laser energy focus on the hair follicles beneath the surface of your skin, rather than burning the hair on the surface.

Avoid Sun Exposure: Sunburned or tanned skin is not suitable for laser hair removal. Avoid sun exposure for at least two weeks before your session.

Skip Certain Products: Stay away from creams, lotions, or other skincare products that could make your skin sensitive. Your technician will provide specific guidelines.

Avoid Waxing or Plucking: In the weeks leading up to your appointment, avoid waxing or plucking the hair in the treatment area. The laser needs the hair follicle to be intact for the procedure to work effectively.

The Full Body Laser Hair

Removal Process

Your full body laser hair removal session at Skintastic will be a breeze. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of what you can expect:

Consultation: Your journey begins with a consultation with one of our experts. They will assess your skin type, hair color, and other factors to determine the most suitable laser settings for your unique needs.

After the session, your skin may appear slightly red or sensitive. However, this is temporary and should subside within a few hours to a day.

Does Full Body

Laser Hair Removal Hurt?

One of the most common concerns about laser hair removal is the pain factor. While everyone’s pain tolerance is different, most people describe the sensation as manageable. It’s often likened to the snap of a rubber band on the skin. The good news is that the sensation is quick, and the results are well worth it.

Aftercare

After your full body laser hair removal session, it’s essential to take care of your skin to ensure the best results. Here are some aftercare tips to follow:

Avoid Sun Exposure: Protect your treated skin from direct sunlight for at least two weeks after the procedure. Use sunscreen with high SPF to shield your skin.

Moisturize: Keep your skin moisturized to prevent dryness or irritation.

Avoid Hot Showers: Steer clear of hot baths or showers for a day or two post-treatment. Opt for lukewarm water instead.

No Scrubbing: Avoid scrubbing the treated area for a few days. Let your skin heal naturally.
